1. Executive Summary
WiseTransact is a mobile application (iOS + Android) that helps individuals build a clean,
categorized record of their financial transactions through the year, so that at tax time they can
hand a CA a comprehensive view of their inflows, outflows, and tax-relevant categorizations
without manually building an Excel sheet from scratch.
The app supports two complementary use cases inside one experience: daily logging for users
who want ongoing visibility into their spend, and episodic catch-up for users who want to
backfill months of data ahead of meeting their CA. Transactions are entered manually or by
pasting bank SMS messages; the app categorizes them, deduplicates intelligently, and produces
both an in-app dashboard and a downloadable year-end PDF report.
The MVP is deliberately scoped as a local-only, free, single-user mobile app with no backend,
no cloud sync, no bank integrations, and no monetization. Anonymized crash reporting and
anonymized behavior analytics are the only egress, both opt-out from Settings and disclosed at
onboarding.
2. Product Vision & Positioning
2.1 Vision
WiseTransact is a quiet, reliable financial record-keeper that turns the year-end "go through
every line of my bank statement" exercise into a problem you've already solved by April 1.

-- 1 of 21 --

2.2 Problem Statement
Indian salaried professionals, freelancers, and self-employed individuals who file their ITR
through a CA are typically asked for payslips and monthly bank statements. The CA expects a
clean, categorized view of inflows and outflows. Today, the user either hands over raw
statements (creating work for the CA) or builds an Excel sheet by manually going through each
transaction line by line — a tedious, error-prone, once-a-year drudgery.
2.3 Target User
Adults in India who file ITR annually through a CA, have one or more bank accounts (savings +
credit card is the typical floor), receive bank transaction alerts via SMS, read English fluently, run
on relatively modern smartphones (Android 12+ or iOS 17+), and are organized enough to want
better records but not so technical that they'd build their own spreadsheet system.
2.4 Positioning Statement
WiseTransact helps you build a clean, categorized record of your expenses
and income through the year, so when tax time comes you and your CA have
full visibility — including expenses and income classifications that matter for
ITR filing. Your data stays only on your device.
Important framing note: WiseTransact is a complement to ITR filing, not a replacement for it.
The app produces a categorized expense-and-income register; it does not file taxes, calculate
liabilities, or provide financial advice.

5. Core User Journeys
5.1 First-time setup
1. User installs and opens the app.
2. Splash screen displays the WiseTransact logo.
3. Onboarding asks for preferred name, then account setup (default "Cash" account pre-
populated; user can keep, rename, remove, or add more).
4. Data-loss warning shown — user acknowledges that data is stored only on this device and
will be lost on uninstall or device change.
5. "Not financial advice" disclaimer shown — one-time acknowledgment.
6. Privacy notice shown — discloses anonymized crash reporting and anonymized behavior
analytics, both default-on, both opt-out from Settings.
7. User lands on the Home screen.
5.2 Daily logging (manual)
1. User opens app → Splash → Home.
2. Taps "+ Add Transaction".
3. Fills the form (Type, Amount, Account, Date, Category, Purpose, Mode, Notes), taps Submit.
4. Dedup check runs. If no duplicate, transaction saves; user returns to Home with confirmation.
5.3 Daily logging (paste SMS)
1. User receives a bank SMS, copies it from messaging app.
2. Opens WiseTransact, taps "+ Add Transaction".
3. Pastes the SMS into the prominent "📋 Paste SMS" field at the top of the form.

-- 4 of 21 --

4. App parses and pre-fills as many fields as it can. Unparseable required fields are flagged.
5. User reviews/edits, fills any required-but-empty fields (e.g., Account if the bank in the SMS
doesn't match an existing account).
6. Taps Submit. Dedup check runs. Transaction saves.
5.4 Year-end CA handoff
1. User opens app, taps "Reports".
2. Changes period picker to "This Financial Year".
3. Reviews in-app charts and totals.
4. Taps "Download PDF". App generates PDF (≤5s), saves to device Downloads, shows
confirmation with file name and Share button.
5. Taps Share. System share sheet opens. User emails or WhatsApps the PDF to their CA.

6.3 Add Transaction [LOCKED]
Layout: Empty form, all fields visible, with a prominent "📋 Paste SMS" action at the top. Pasting
an SMS auto-populates the form below it. Manual users can ignore the paste action and fill the
form directly.
Field order:
1. Type — three-button toggle: Income / Expense / Transfer. Default: Expense. Credit/Debit is
intentionally not shown to the user.
2. Amount — numeric input. Required. Must be > 0.
3. Account — picker. For Income/Expense: single Account. For Transfer: From-Account + To-
Account. Required. Defaults to user's most-used account.
4. Date — picker. Defaults to today. Allows today and any past date within the current FY; future
dates not allowed in MVP.
5. Category — dropdown. Income → Salary / Interest / Freelance / Rental / Other (or custom).
Expense → Food / Transport / Healthcare / etc. (or custom). Transfer → not applicable. Rule-
based auto-suggest fills this when a known merchant is identified.
6. Purpose — Expense only: Personal / Professional / Investment / Tax-saving. Default:
Personal. Not shown for Income or Transfer.
7. Mode — UPI / Card / Cash / Bank Transfer / Other.
8. Notes — optional free-text.
9. Submit — disabled until all required fields are populated.
Edit behavior: Every auto-extracted field is fully editable. The user has final say on every field,
always.
Large-amount soft warning: Amount ≥ ₹10,00,000 (₹10 lakh) → non-blocking confirmation
prompt: "This is a large amount (₹X). Are you sure?" with [Yes] / [Edit].
Zero or negative amount: Inline error on the Amount field; Submit blocked.
6.4 SMS Parsing & Auto-Population [LOCKED]
Scope: Single-SMS paste only in MVP. Bulk SMS paste deferred.
Language: English-only parsing.
Multiple-amount SMS: Keyword-proximity heuristic — amounts adjacent to "debited",
"credited", "spent", "received" win; amounts adjacent to "balance", "avl bal", "available" are
ignored.

Parse-failure ladder: Full success → all fields pre-filled. Partial success → empty/uncertain
fields highlighted. Parse failure → friendly inline message asking the user to enter manually.
Account auto-population: If SMS bank doesn't match any existing account, Account stays
empty and required. App does not auto-create accounts.
6.5 Categorization & Auto-suggest [LOCKED]
Rule-based auto-categorization. The app maintains a library of merchant-keyword → category
mappings. For SMS-pasted transactions where a merchant is detected, the app suggests a
Category. The user can always accept, override, or pick a custom category.
Custom categories: user-specific, do not participate in the merchant rule library. Where
possible, when a user types a custom category, the UI suggests the closest predefined match.
Purpose tags (Expense only): Personal / Professional / Investment / Tax-saving. Default
Personal.
6.6 Deduplication [LOCKED]
Approach: Conservative — always prompt; never silently merge.
When dedup runs: On every Submit, regardless of input method.
Match criteria (all must be true): Same Account, same Type, Amount within ±1% or ±₹1
(whichever is larger), Date within ±1 day.

10. Privacy, Legal & Disclaimers [LOCKED]
10.1 Privacy posture
All transaction data, account names, preferred name, and any other personal data is stored on
the user's device only, in the app's persistent local database. No transaction data, account
names, or preferred name is ever transmitted off the device. The only network egress is
anonymized crash reports and anonymized behavior analytics — covered in §10.4.
10.2 Required documents
Privacy Policy — public web page, linked from Settings and store listings. Discloses local-
only data storage, plus anonymized crash reporting and anonymized behavior analytics in
plain language.
Terms of Service / EULA — public web page, standard boilerplate, linked from Settings.
DPDP Act compliance statement — included in Privacy Policy. States that no transaction
data, financial data, or personally identifying information is processed off the device.
10.3 Disclaimers
"Not financial advice" — first-launch acknowledgment + Settings access.
PDF report footer — "Generated by WiseTransact based on user-entered data. This report is
for personal record-keeping. Verify all entries with original bank statements and consult a
qualified Chartered Accountant before tax filing."
SMS input format — inline note on SMS-paste field: "Currently supports English SMS
messages from Indian banks. Other formats may not parse correctly."

10.4 Crash reporting & behavior analytics
The app uses two anonymized data streams to support product quality and improvement. Both
default to on, both are disclosed at onboarding, and both have independent opt-out toggles in
Settings.
Crash reporting (e.g., Firebase Crashlytics or Sentry — engineering's choice): captures stack
traces, device model, OS version, app version, and crash context when the app crashes.
Behavior analytics (privacy-respecting platform such as PostHog, Plausible, or Aptabase —
engineering's choice; constraint: no PII, no transaction data, no account names, no preferred
name, no advertising SDKs): captures aggregated, anonymous event counts that power the
success metrics in §12 — for example, "user opened Add Transaction screen", "user generated
PDF", "user added a transaction (manual or SMS)". Every event is anonymous and tied only to a
randomly-generated, non-identifying device ID used solely to compute distinct-user counts.
Strict constraints on both streams:
Never include transaction amounts, merchant names, account names, preferred name, notes,
or category names.
Never include any user-typed content.
Never include any data that could re-identify a user across devices.
Privacy Policy must disclose in plain language: what is sent, what is not sent, why it is sent, the
third-party processor, and how to opt out.

12. Success Metrics [LOCKED]
Behavior analytics is in scope for MVP per §10.4. All metrics below are
technically measurable from day one. Targets are v1 ambitions and should be
re-baselined after the first 90 days of real data.
12.1 North Star Metric
Why this is the North Star: WiseTransact's primary risk in MVP is not "will users tell their CA
about it" but "will users get over the hump of logging their first transaction." Activation is the
leading indicator that all downstream value flows depend on. It will most clearly tell engineering
and product whether the onboarding-to-first-transaction path actually works.
12.2 Primary Metric
Definition of "active": opened the app and either viewed a screen with data, added a
transaction, or generated a report. A pure splash-and-close session does not count.
Why WAU + MAU: WAU gives a tight feedback loop appropriate for an app users are expected
to interact with multiple times per week. MAU is a sanity check for users who batch-log monthly
and would otherwise look "inactive" by WAU alone.
